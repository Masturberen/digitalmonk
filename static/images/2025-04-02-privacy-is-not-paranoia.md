Privacy isn't about hiding. It's about choosing.

Somewhere along the way, the internet convinced us that the price of convenience was transparency. Your location, your search history, your heartbeat, your purchase patterns — all of it siphoned quietly into data centres owned by companies with a legal obligation to shareholders, not to you.

This is not conspiracy thinking. It's the business model, stated plainly in terms of service nobody reads.

## The Surveillance Stack

Here's what the average person runs without thinking about it: Chrome (collects browsing data tied to your Google account), Gmail (scanned for ad targeting), Google Search (every query logged and profiled), WhatsApp (metadata collected by Meta), Windows with telemetry enabled.

None of these are secrets. They're just defaults.

## Open Source Alternatives

You don't need to go off-grid. You need to shift defaults.

**BROWSER:** Firefox with uBlock Origin installed. Or better: LibreWolf, a hardened Firefox fork with privacy settings pre-configured. No telemetry, no sponsored shortcuts.

**SEARCH:** SearXNG (self-hosted meta-search) or Startpage (proxies Google without logging). Brave Search is also decent. Other options: DuckDuckGo and Ecosia.

**EMAIL:** ProtonMail (Switzerland, end-to-end encrypted, open source client) or Tutanota. Both have free tiers. Aliases via SimpleLogin or addy.io mean you never give your real address to a service again.

**MESSAGING:** Signal. This is the one. Open source, end-to-end encrypted by default, minimal metadata.

**OPERATING SYSTEM:** Linux is not as scary as it was in 2008. Pop!_OS and Fedora both install in 20 minutes and look modern. No telemetry, no ads in the start menu, no forced updates. Personally I use Arch Linux and [EndeavourOS](https://endeavouros.com/) for playing games with Steam.

**PASSWORDS:** Bitwarden. Open source, audited, free tier is genuinely complete. Stop reusing passwords. For authentication I use [ente.io/auth](https://ente.io/auth/).

**DNS:** Switch your DNS to Quad9 (9.9.9.9) or NextDNS. This blocks malware and tracking at the network level.

## The Argument People Make

"I have nothing to hide."

This argument assumes the only reason for privacy is guilt. But you close the bathroom door. You don't hand your diary to strangers. You don't broadcast your bank PIN. Privacy is not about wrongdoing — it's about having a self that exists outside of a database.

Data collected today will exist for decades. The company that seems benign now may be acquired, hacked, subpoenaed, or simply change its policies.

## Where to Start

If you do nothing else: install uBlock Origin in your browser today. It takes 30 seconds and immediately blocks thousands of trackers across every site you visit. It's free, open source, and maintained by volunteers.

Then, when you have time: switch your search engine. Then your email. Small steps. The goal isn't perfect privacy — that's impossible. The goal is reducing surface area.
