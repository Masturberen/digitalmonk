Today I picked up a custom made bicycle which is a bit of a Frankenstein — a custom cargo-fork, BMX handlebars and a 90s mountain bike frame. In a rusty color.

End of last year I approached a guy online that creates custom bicycles as a hobby. Before he used to tinker around with motorcycles. You would think that's probably his occupation. But no — the man is a graphic designer.

After a two hour train ride to pick up my "post apocalyptic solar punk bike" he came outside to welcome me, showed me the bike. I was amazed by what he created. Some years ago I did my own poor attempt, but I'm not very technical with building stuff, let alone mechanical things.

I got some coffee and sweet cookies made by his wife. She even invited me to stay for dinner. I kindly refused.

Their kids, around the age of seven or nine, were playing some video game on the television in the living room. I also noticed they had a big grey bunny in a cage there. After some chitchatting about graphic design, riso prints, and bike stuff, he adjusted some things and showed me what he did to the build. Out of all things I was kinda surprised he added solar panels, so I could technically charge my phone when the sun is shining bright. I thought this was such a fun touch.

After giving him the money and thanking them, I left and biked to the station.

## The Train Back

My phone acted up and had no internet connection, but eventually I connected to the Wi-Fi hotspot of the train. Once on the train with the bike, I read for a bit. Later ate a banana the lady gave me for on the way home.

When I transferred to another train, another guy came in with his bike. Right away complimenting mine. So we chatted a bit — told him about the build, he talked about visiting his parents and picking up his bike there since his previous one fell apart. We talked a bit about the state of the world and our goals and plans.

It's been a while I had a conversation with a stranger on public transport, without even exchanging names or socials whatsoever. Very refreshing to have this experience after a long time.
